User Manual

Helm is the executive dashboard for the DockLabs suite. It rolls up real-time metrics, action items, and alerts from every peer product (Admiralty, Beacon, Bounty, Harbor, Lookout, Manifest, Purser, Yeoman) into a single view.

Helm also hosts a lightweight project-management surface (Tasks) for cross-cutting work that doesn't fit any single peer product — interagency coordination, FOIA case management, and capital improvement plans.

Today is your personal stand-up. Three columns:

  • My Work — your open tasks grouped Overdue / Today / This week / Upcoming.
  • Awaiting Me — items across the suite where you are the gating dependency (signing steps in Manifest, FOIA assignments in Admiralty, review queues in Harbor or Purser, etc.).
  • Alerts — local notifications, unread peer notifications, and (staff only) ops canaries.

Across the suite is the executive view: fleet metric cards with sparklines, an aggregate action queue, aggregate alerts, and per-product drill-down panels you can expand inline.

Helm asks each peer product through a per-user inbox endpoint (/api/v1/helm-feed/inbox/). The peer resolves your DockLabs identity to its local user record and returns the items where you are the open assignee, signer, reviewer, or owner — with deep links and due dates.

If a peer hasn't enabled the per-user endpoint yet (or it's offline), Helm falls back to a coarse count from that peer's aggregate feed and renders a ~N badge with a tooltip. Nothing else is affected.

  1. Open Tasks → Projects, click New project, pick a kind (Standard, FOIA, or CIP), set the name and target date.
  2. On the project page, click Claim. You become the LEAD.
  3. Click Collaborators, pick a user (or enter an email), assign a role: LEAD, CONTRIBUTOR, REVIEWER, or OBSERVER.
  4. The invitee receives a helm_collaborator_invited notification.

Only LEAD and CONTRIBUTOR can edit tasks. REVIEWER can comment but not edit. OBSERVER is read-only.

Projects with kind = FOIA carry a statutory clock. Connecticut (CGS §1-206 — 4 business days to acknowledge) is the default; Federal (5 USC 552 — 20 business days) is also supported.

The deadline is computed from the received_at date, the jurisdiction, and any tolling windows you record.

  • Toll pauses the clock — use when waiting on a fee agreement or requester clarification.
  • Untoll resumes it. The deadline shifts forward by the tolled duration and is recomputed automatically.

A Capital Improvement Plan (CIP) project tracks federal-fund-tied infrastructure work. The kind unlocks:

  • Fund sources — structured entries for ARPA, IIJA, IRA, BEAD, SLCGP, CDBG, GO Bond, Revenue Bond, and match dollars.
  • Federal compliance flags — Davis-Bacon, Build America Buy America, NEPA, and state/local environmental review.

These flags are advisory — they document your audit posture. They don't enforce blocking rules.

When a project's visibility is set to Public (LEAD-only toggle), it becomes reachable at /p/<public_id>/ with no authentication. The public page shows:

  • Project name, status, and target dates.
  • Task completion percentage.
  • Fund sources and totals (CIP only).
  • The FOIA statutory deadline countdown (FOIA only).

It does not expose notes, attachments, collaborators, comments, or any PII.

From a project page, click Summarize. Helm calls Claude to produce a short narrative covering current status, recent activity, blockers, and upcoming deadlines.

Summaries are cached. The first request returns the cached version; click Refresh to regenerate. Each summary is stamped with the model version and the time it was generated.

Open your sidebar profile dropdown and click Notification Preferences (or visit /notifications/preferences/).

For each event type, toggle the in-app and email channels independently. Critical alerts are delivered regardless of preferences.

Staff users see four ops canaries on the dashboard's Alerts panel, pulled live from /api/v1/metrics/:

  • audit_silent_24h — no AuditLog rows in 24 hours.
  • cron_silent_24h — no scheduled-job runs in 24 hours.
  • cron_failures_24h — at least one job errored.
  • notifications_failing — at least one delivery failed.

For per-job detail, visit the Scheduled jobs dashboard.
